OUR STORY

"Therabreath sets out the goal to develop the first alcoholic drink that will bring benefits to oral health. It's not just an outrageous idea, it's a revolution" — Ryan Reynolds

01THE ORIGIN

It has become a trend for companies to develop products that hold health benefits to meet the demands of consumers on this aspect. Since May 2019, the brewery group Anheuser-Busch InBev (AB InBev) – well known for creating Budweiser and other beers, had been developing on a product that focused on features which revolved around consumers’ health. The idea was to create a beer that would make people gargle and enjoyed at the same time. One of the proposals stood out, which was to create a product, that combined of fresh beer and a product that worked as good as a mouth lozenge.

02THE PROCESS

Several approaches were adapted into reality, but every one of them ended up with a bad result. It was recognized that a new approach and a corporation between AB InBev and a well-known company which focuses on oral care products must be established. After a careful consideration and a prolonged researching process, AB InBev had finally formed an outstandingly suitable partnership with Therabreath – a brand of oral care products headquartered in Los Angeles, California.

03THE PARTNERSHIP

Two companies had decided to come together and work on a new beer product that would treat issues related to halitosis – also known as bad breath, a symptom in which a noticeably unpleasant breath odor is present. It did not take long for both companies to conduct a detailed research and developed several possible products as experiments. In January 2020, it was finalized that two companies had come up with a beer on the market that would cure halitosis and bring oral care benefits to consumers.

04THE RESULT

In January 2020, Therabreath Beer was released and became a huge success on the beer market. Therabreath Beer has several flavours, which all leave different unique tastes, but also maintain the slight bitterness and much sweeter of Budweiser’s well-known flavor. The release of Therabreath Beer also marks the first event in the history of the brewing industry of having a beer that focuses on oral health.
